Two Weeks in Quarantine (So Far)

2020-04-06 — Written by Dillon de Silva
#covid-19  #quarantine 

Initial Thoughts

It goes without saying that COVID-19 has significantly affected all of our lives. A widespread pandemic spreading waves of uncertainty around our social wellbeing, work and economy radiates through the Earth right now.

Two weeks ago, I began my share of self isolation. Whilst it’s one thing to be alone, being forced to be alone is of an entirely new experience to me.

Like many other people, I initially assumed quarantine was going to be somewhat an easy experience. However, five days in, I found myself frustrated at the situation. Not having as much freedom and the constant negative news stream infesting within my mind have been the most detrimental outcomes out of the situation for me.

What Changed for Me

After some time (around the tenth day), the situation actually started beginning to ease for me. I began to settle into my online schooling regime and have found extra time to get ahead of my studies. In addition, I have also been able to keep up with a variety of my hobbies and take up a few new ones as well. I find myself playing more guitar, writing more code and working on my various art and crafts more. I can say that this is helping to keep my sanity and I am glad.

The severity of any global pandemic should not be ignored. COVID-19 is an unknown pathogen after all so being precautious and following the self isolation guidelines is important.

However, if you are struggling I hope to inspire you or give you some ideas as to how you may be able to get through this tough period of time.

Evaluate Yourself

What are your strengths? What are your soft spots? What can you do to be a better person when the world begins to reset back to its “original state”. Sometimes having time out is the best time to understand yourself and to grow as a person.

Be Grateful

It’s the little things which can mean the most in life. I miss the simple hellos I could give out to my friends at school or members of the community. I miss laughing with my mates and making nonsense jokes as if nothing in the world could possibly matter more.

Be grateful for the people in your lives and the little things you have. Be grateful for the things you have. Clearly recent events have showed us that so much can be taken away from us in an instant.

Aim to never waste a moment of your precious life.

Make a Post-Quarantine Bucket List

Making a post quarantine bucket list believe it or not is actually a great way to explore the future. Be adventurous! Right down the most outright crazy things you want to do in life after coronavirus. Even if you don’t get around to completing them, a little contemplation about the future is a great way to kill some time in self isolation.

Family

Without a doubt, quarantine has opened up more time for family members to be with one another. Assuming they don’t drive you crazy, this is a great opportunity to get to know one another on an even deeper level and spend time with the people you live with. Spending lunchtimes, goofing around with my dog and sister as well as having good conversations with my parents is helping me to keep my sanity and get to know them even better than I already did.

Hobbies

Now is an amazing time to rebuild yourself and learn new things! Pick a skill your interested in and then you can do at home and…

LEARN IT!

Whether its mastering a musical instrument, learning poetry, sewing or mastering a branch of mathematics in a week, there has never been a better time to learn new things.

Personally, I’ve increased the amount of reading I am doing and have plans to pick up a new language this week. I think it’s a really good way for me to get some of my mind off crunching mathematical equations and engage my creative senses a bit more.

Spread Love and Care Around the World

Message the ones you rarely talk to. Give close family members a call. Spread love and positive vibes to everyone in your life right now.

We’ve all been impacted in some way and right now the best thing we can all do for each other (apart from a little social distancing) is giving love and caring for one another. It’s where we as human beings can have the potential to thrive
