Mothers

2020-04-22
#poetry 

To say I am blessed
By the sturdy, soft branches
Of my giver of life
Would be a bold understatement

An unsung hero she is
Having endured a blizzard of knives
Whilst ever so carefully
Upholding unripened fruit above her head

I am privileged to breathe fresh air
Yet prone to forget
That such rich concoction of chemicals
Is the foundation upon which my many neurons
Fire with such intensity
Powering the body in which I take form

I envy her strength
A fierce kindness bestowed within her heart
Igniting her every move
In an effort for my great, joyful days

A warm, warm smile
And compassion much more prudent
Than the vivid contrast of clove pink carnations
At which I was born to

As she ages
So do I
Yet timeless memories of food, fun, knowledge and laughter
Remain forever imprisoned within my head

It is true
That she is symbolic of nature
And it is nature I have to thank
For the waters, the land and the air
At which I have learnt the volume I know today
And continue to learn so

A thank you is unsatisfactory to say the least
For by my mother’s hand and mothers alike
The soft spirits of children like myself
Continue to flourish with nutrients of every kind

So I leave these words
Not just upon my nurturer
But as an open expression of gratitude
For all the nurturers favouring the world
