Why I Decided to Combine Maths, Art and Code

2020-05-26 — Written by Dillon de Silva
#Art  #Creativity  #Ideas 

I’ve always had cross over passions for many different things. Whether it’s music or hacking away at a weekend project, a desire to use my creative strengths and see things from a new angle is one of the biggest motivators in terms of learning new things for me.

My latest development in terms of creative projects has had me venturing into territory using both the left and right hand side of my brain :)

A Gosper Curve

So back track to about three months ago…

My brain is out. I’m out of ideas. I haven’t written any actual useful code in so long (my Github contributions chart also agrees with this statement :p ).

I need a creative release.

After discussing this issue with one of my colleagues (for the purposes of this article, we shall refer to them as Ben), we started flicking through some of my old projects. Scavenging for any half complete projects or inspiration for new ideas.

Whilst doing this, I found an image I’d generated using a library called p5.js and some mathematical formulas. Puzzled by the beauty of the piece, I showed it to Ben and asked for his opinion on it. Fascinated by the beauty of the artwork itself as well as the creative process involved in developing it, we began discussing the logistics of potentially developing more of these images.

A growing passion for art and the need for canvases to paint the walls of bedroom got me thinking…

What if I can develop artworks using maths and code?

Now I am most certainly not the first person to attempt this. I researched several concepts relating to these ideas including:

  • Generative Artworks
  • Lindenmayer Systems
  • Graphs of Trigonometric Functions
  • Fractals (Mandelbrot, Julia, Koch, etc)

After conducting several experiments and working hard to research and implement new algorithms and formulas into programs which deliver stunning visuals, I managed to generate some intriguing images.

Manipulating trigonometric functions and adding some colour gave me this cool shape

By varying these images using different shapes or composition elements as well new algorithms and fractals, my artworks only managed to get more interesting.

An artistic representation of a Gosper Curve

Since then, I have been designing and developing several artworks bridging maths, art and code together.

My artworks will be visible and available on the 30th of May for purchase from my Redbubble site!

https://www.redbubble.com/people/poppyboi/

Combining three abstract things I’m super passionate about and adding in inspiration from various colour schemes, existing ideas and a ludicrous amount of experimentation, I’ve been able to channel a once lost idea into a new creative passion.

Main takeaways from this for everyone:

  • Be creative and find that drive to be invent
  • Research and learn more. The more you learn, the better ideas will come.
  • You can paint a picture with a computer (literally and metaphorically)
  • Don’t stop looking for ideas. Even when you’ve hit rock bottom with your brain, I can guarantee you something amazing is within your reach that you haven’t thought of
  • Don’t be afraid to cross branches. Who said you need to stick to being interested in only a specific field?

Love you all :) Hope this has inspired you in some way! Get in touch with me and let me know!
