isolated.

2020-04-10 — Written by Dillon de Silva
#poetry  #covid-19  #quarantine 

Heroes are forged in caves
Within one’s deepest desolation
The good witch’s brewery opens

A cauldron to craft potions
Ingredients of love and gratitude
Wand to paper, light to nature

Now as the devil reigns temporarily
It is important to confine
But never let your mind
Fall short, six feet behind

Though I seem somewhat clever
My oasis is but a replica of yours
Navigating survival amidst the new evolution

To offer my hand
Would be somewhat unwise
So succumb to my spirit
Let the cord unwind

For should you choose to throw it
Over the damned city walls
It shall not hit the bottom of the ocean
As scientists once theorised
But rather it will travel endlessly
Through a sky of rainbow clouds

I crave the day this reality ends
Yet to excessively slum
Would be my demise

So within my cave I lay
Forging, forging, forging
To strike the sun than to be stricken by it
When it rises once again
As our eyes look to the East
